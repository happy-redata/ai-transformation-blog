We have all heard of viruses *on* the computer. But what if the virus *is* the computer?

## The Scenario: The Fake Mac

Imagine this:
Your company orders 10 new MacBooks for management. They arrive in nice white boxes, wrapped in plastic. Everything looks perfect.

But one of them has taken a detour.

Between the factory and your office, it has been in the hands of a "modern Trojan". They opened it up, and under the battery, they soldered a tiny extra chip onto the motherboard. A 4G modem no bigger than a fingernail.

The <Voice text="Zero Trust">Zero Trust</Voice> team sets up the machine. They install antivirus. They disable Wi-Fi. They isolate it on a secure network. They think they are safe.

<Speaker voice="narrator">
But the horse is already inside.
</Speaker>

While the CEO reads secret documents on the screen, that little chip is sending screenshots directly out via the 4G network. Bypassing your firewall. Bypassing your security. The machine is literally phoning home.

## The Solution: The Faraday Cage

How do you protect against a spy living inside the hardware itself? You cannot use software to find it.

You must use physics.

We call it a **Faraday Cage**. It is a room lined with copper mesh that blocks all radio signals. No Wi-Fi, no Bluetooth, and most importantly: **No 4G/5G**.

<SoundEffect name="success-chime" />

If you work with state secrets or patent blueprints, this is where you sit.

1.  **No signals out:** The spy chip screams into the void. It cannot send data home.
2.  **No signals in:** No one can remote control the machine from outside.
